Privacy Policy

Daily Timer for Notion  ·  Last updated: April 30, 2026

1. Overview

Daily Timer for Notion is a browser extension that runs entirely within your browser. We do not operate any backend server, and we do not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external systems.

2. Data we access

  • Notion page content (DOM): when you click "Load list", the extension reads the column headers or row names visible in the currently open Notion board tab. This data never leaves your browser — it is used only to populate the participant list in the timer UI.
  • Notion API (optional): if you choose to configure a Notion Integration Token and Database ID in the extension settings, the extension will call the official Notion API (api.notion.com) on your behalf to retrieve page titles and task counts. Your token is stored locally in Chrome's sync storage and is sent only to Notion's own servers. We never receive or store it.
  • Timer state: the running/paused state, participant list, and elapsed time are stored in Chrome's session storage (cleared when you close the browser) so the timer survives page reloads.

3. Data we do NOT collect

  • We do not collect names, emails, or any personally identifiable information.
  • We do not send any data to our own servers — we have none.
  • We do not use analytics, tracking pixels, or cookies.
  • We do not sell, share, or disclose any data to third parties.

4. Third-party services

The optional API mode communicates with Notion using your own credentials. Please refer to Notion's Privacy Policy for information about how Notion handles data.

This extension is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Notion Labs, Inc.

5. Permissions used

  • storage – to save your settings (API token, database ID) and timer state locally.
  • tabs – to detect which Notion tab is active and inject the sidebar timer.
  • scripting – to inject the timer panel into Notion pages.
  • https://www.notion.so/* – to read the DOM of Notion pages (participant names).
  • https://api.notion.com/* – to call the Notion API when the API mode is configured.

6. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
